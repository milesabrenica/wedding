Benjamin & Miles

about us

There is never a dull moment when she's around because she is always laughing, even when there's no joke. She has the same dance move for every song. Her backyard is a zoo. Animals roam around as though they own the place. She names EVERYTHING Kevin. She can memorize lyrics to any song in the first couple times hearing it. Miles is a person who loves unconditionally and without hesitation. She sees the best in people because she believes everyone is beautiful in their own little way. She's the joy of my life. I can't live without her and I'm honored and privileged to call her my future wife.

We've been friends since we were kids growing up in the same congregation. Our relationship always consisted of making fun of each other (until one of us got upset - usually Miles), and a ridiculous amount of selfies during the pre-iPhone days. We never thought in a million years that we would end up together. It wasn't until one weekend in San Diego, after talking, body boarding all day and throwing a football around for an hour that we started to see each other in a different light. For days after, we were inseperable. We quickly became the best of friends that we still are today.

He's the king of corny, listens to Colbie Caillat while working out, can't memorize one song (okay fine, you memorized "You are my Sunshine"), plays hide and seek ALL THE TIME, and has crooked eyelashes. He has a heart full of love that I still can't comprehend, and the strength to make anyone feel safe even through the roughest times. He's proof that chivalry is not dead and a good guy is not that hard to find. He's the best friend I've ever had and I look forward to slow dancing in the kitchen with him every night while making dinner for the rest of our lives.

how he asked

By 9:00am I was kidnapped. He had my girls take me around town, following clues that were handed to me by restaurant workers with some kind of dessert. Each clue was a hand written letter of reminders of his love, and that we eat way too much! My very last clue led us to Reseda Point. The best view of the valley.

As I walked up the hill, already sobbing and out of breath, I turn the corner and see 30 of our friends and family lined up along a red aisle, which at the end read, "Happily Ever After Starts Here". And there he was. Standing right by those words, he waited for me to walk through the support that surrounded us.

He first asked me to marry him with a Ring Pop, (it was in it's own box and everything!) and I immediately said "YES!" I threw my arms in the air. While I was busy having my own 5 second dance party, he got back down on one knee and asked me again. This time, with a much shinier and durable ring than the first. And the rest is history!
